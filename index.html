What is the Clarke County Mentor Program?

It takes one person to make a difference in the life of a child.

About the Program

The Clarke County Mentor Program is an organization that employs volunteers, who are at least 18, to be mentors. These mentors will be assigned to a child at a local school in the county. The main focus of the program is to match up a friend who provides support, encouragement, and guidance with a young person. This program proves to be a rewarding experience not only for the child, but for the mentor as well. Check out the Clarke County Mentor Program Website for more information!

My Experience with the Program

I decided to become a mentor mainly to try to get involved in the community. I wanted to use this opportunity as just another volunteer work that I could add to my resume. However, I soon realized that this experience would be one that I cherish forever. This program gave me the potential to change a young person's life. Having a mentee forces me to stop thinking about myself on a consistent basis. I put my energy into helping someone reach their future goals and overcome their fears and they have helped me to see that my problems were not insurmountable. Becoming a mentor has lead to a series of events and opportunities that I would never had before.
Getting involved has forced me to learn, evolve, and overall, become a better person.

How to Get Involved

  1. Make sure you have a free hour on a specific day of the week that you can devote to your assigned child.
  2. Be able to be a mentor for a year from the time you start (not including breaks).
  3. Fill out the application and be as informative as possible!
  4. Go to a training session at a time that works for you!
